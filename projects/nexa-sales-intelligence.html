Analysis

Key Findings

The dashboard revealed three structural performance issues that together formed the case for strategic intervention.

01
Revenue Collapse of 62.1%
Revenue peaked at $4.72M in 2004 before falling sharply to $1.79M in 2005 — a 62.1% decline, and 2005 data only covers five months (Jan–May), suggesting the underlying trend was even more severe than the figures indicate.
02
Dangerous Product Concentration
Classic Cars alone generated $3.92M — 39.1% of total revenue. With just one product line carrying nearly 40% of revenue, any market disruption to that category poses an existential risk to NEXA's financial performance.
03
$440K Revenue at Risk
Cancelled, On Hold, and Disputed orders account for over $440,000 in potential lost revenue — 4.4% of the total. Systemic fulfilment and customer relationship issues are likely drivers of this recoverable loss.
04
USA Dominates at 36.2%
The United States accounts for $3.63M — 36.2% of total revenue. North America as a territory is NEXA's backbone, while APAC and Japan remain significantly underpenetrated relative to their market potential.
05
November is Peak Month
November consistently generates the highest monthly revenue ($2.12M across all years), followed by October — pointing to Q4 seasonal demand cycles. Revenue in June and July is significantly lower, indicating a predictable summer trough.
06
Large Deals Driven by Volume
The Key Influencers visual confirms that higher total units sold increases the likelihood of a Large deal size by 5.36×. Classic Cars specifically make a deal 3.10× more likely to be classified as Large — reinforcing both a growth pathway and a concentration risk.

Strategic Recommendations

What NEXA Should Do

Based on the dashboard analysis, six evidence-based recommendations were developed to address NEXA's structural vulnerabilities.

  • 📦
    Diversify product portfolio beyond Classic Cars
    Classic Cars at 39.1% is a systemic single-point-of-failure. NEXA should invest marketing and distribution capacity in Vintage Cars and Motorcycles — both have demonstrated strong unit volumes — to reduce concentration risk to below 25% for any single line.
  • 🌏
    Aggressively expand APAC and Japan presence
    Asia Pacific and Japan together contribute less than 10% of revenue despite being large collector markets. Partnering with local distributors in Singapore, the Philippines, and Australia (all existing markets) would be a lower-risk entry strategy with proven demand signals.
  • 🔧
    Resolve fulfilment gaps causing $440K in lost revenue
    A root-cause audit of Cancelled and On Hold orders should be the first operational priority. Addressing payment terms, shipping logistics, or credit disputes is likely to unlock a meaningful portion of that revenue without requiring new customers.
  • 📅
    Build a seasonal demand strategy around Q4 peaks
    November and October consistently outperform all other months. NEXA should pre-position inventory, pre-negotiate bulk shipments, and run targeted promotions in June–August to smooth the summer revenue trough and reduce quarterly volatility.
  • 🤝
    Prioritise large-deal customer relationships
    Large deals ($1.3M total) carry the highest revenue per transaction. The data confirms large deals are driven by volume orders on Classic Cars. A key account management programme targeting NEXA's top 20 customers by order size would protect and grow this segment.
  • 📊
    Investigate the 2004–2005 revenue collapse drivers
    The 62.1% decline from 2004 to 2005 is not explained by the current dataset alone. Supplementing with cost, margin, and customer churn data would allow a full diagnostic. Until then, all strategic decisions carry uncertainty about whether the decline reflects market, operational, or data-collection factors.

Visualisations Used

Dashboard Design Choices

Each chart type was selected to match the analytical question it answers — avoiding generic defaults in favour of advanced Power BI visuals.

🗺 Map Chart
Geographically plots 19 countries by bubble size (revenue), giving executives an instant global footprint view without reading tables.
🌳 Treemap
Encodes product line revenue as proportional rectangles. Classic Cars' dominance becomes visually self-evident — impossible to miss or misread.
📉 Waterfall Chart
Shows year-on-year revenue change with increase/decrease/total bars. The 2005 collapse reads as a dramatic downward step — far more impactful than a line.
🔽 Funnel Chart
Visualises revenue by order status from Shipped (largest) down through disputed and in-process orders — directly quantifying the fulfilment efficiency gap.
🎯 Key Influencers
Power BI's AI visual identifies what factors most increase the probability of a Large deal size — revealing the 5.36× volume multiplier and the Classic Cars effect.
⚡ Scatter Plot
Plots units ordered against revenue for each product line — immediately showing which lines achieve revenue efficiency (high value per unit) vs. high volume but lower price.
