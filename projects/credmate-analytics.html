Company Overview

About Credmate

Credmate is a Nigerian digital lending platform operating in the microfinance and fintech sector. Its model sits at the intersection of data-driven risk assessment and accessible credit — offering short-to-medium-term loans to individuals across a broad spectrum of employment types, from civil servants and salary earners to traders and artisans.

The business partners with established lenders including Renmoney, FairMoney, Carbon, Branch, PalmCredit, and KiaKia, distributing credit across major Nigerian states. This analysis uses data from the Eazee MFB dataset, which captures 1,000 loan records spanning 2024–2025, covering loan disbursements, repayment outcomes, borrower demographics, credit scores, and lender relationships.

The objective of this dashboard is to give Credmate's risk officers and business analysts a single source of truth for portfolio health — enabling them to identify where losses are concentrated, which borrower segments present the highest risk, and where collection strategy should be intensified.

Analysis

Key Findings

⚠️ 55% of the Portfolio is At Risk

Combined, non-performing (₦25.8M), written-off (₦7.1M), and delinquent (₦3.7M) loans account for over half of total disbursements. Only ₦0.02M in loans are currently active and on track, pointing to a fundamental gap between disbursement velocity and collection capability.

🏢 Self-Employed Borrowers Drive Defaults

Power BI's Key Influencers AI model found that self-employed applicants are 1.28× more likely to become non-performing than any other employment segment. This group typically lacks payslip verification and has volatile income — making traditional credit scoring metrics less reliable.

🎓 Students & Salary Earners Are Underserved

Students and salary earners cluster in the top-right of the credit score vs repayment scatter — meaning they have both high creditworthiness AND strong repayment behaviour. Yet these segments represent a small share of the portfolio. Credmate could safely grow here.

📍 Ogun & Osun Show Regional Concentration Risk

The geospatial map reveals that Ogun (₦1.82M NP) and Osun (₦1.79M NP) carry the highest non-performing balances relative to their portfolio sizes. This may indicate weak local collection agents, economic shocks, or borrower profile mismatches in those states.

📱 Phone Purchase Loans Have Mixed Outcomes

Phone purchase appears across both the non-performing and written-off buckets in the decomposition tree and waterfall chart. While popular, these asset-backed loans are difficult to recover — a phone depreciates rapidly and is easily hidden. Medical and school fees loans show better recovery patterns.

Business Recommendations

Strategic Recommendations for Credmate

01
Restrict Self-Employed Loan Limits

Cap loan amounts for self-employed applicants until income verification is strengthened. Introduce bank statement analysis or agency-verified cashflow checks before approving loans above ₦50,000 to this segment.

02
Grow the Salary Earner Segment

Salary earners and students show the best repayment behaviour. Credmate should develop employer-linked payroll deduction products for this segment, reducing collection friction and default risk simultaneously.

03
Deploy Field Agents in Ogun & Osun

The geographic concentration of non-performing loans in Ogun and Osun warrants dedicated recovery agents in these states. Consider partnering with local cooperative societies or community leaders to improve borrower contact rates.

04
Re-evaluate Phone Purchase Products

Phone purchase loans appear in both the NP and written-off buckets. Credmate should either require IMEI registration as collateral, shorten loan tenors for device purchases, or introduce device insurance as a loan condition.

05
Monitor Renmoney & Carbon Exposure

The lender exposure treemap shows Renmoney and Carbon carrying the largest share of the non-performing book. Renegotiate risk-sharing terms with these lenders or rebalance disbursements toward better-performing partnerships like Branch and PalmCredit.

06
Tighten Q1 Disbursement Pace

The monthly trend shows Q1 2025 had the highest disbursement volume but flat repayment rates — new cohorts may be lower quality. Credmate should implement cohort-level performance gates: if a monthly cohort's 30-day repayment rate falls below 60%, slow new approvals immediately.
