Analysis

Key Findings

The dashboard revealed five structural credit quality issues that form the basis for strategic intervention.

01
55% of Portfolio is At Risk
Non-performing (₦25.8M), written-off (₦7.1M), and delinquent (₦3.7M) loans together account for over half of total disbursements. Only 0.1% of loans are currently active and performing — a severe collection bottleneck.
02
Self-Employed Borrowers Drive Defaults
Power BI's Key Influencers AI model identified self-employed applicants as 1.28× more likely to become non-performing than any other segment. Volatile income and lack of payslip verification make credit scoring unreliable for this group.
03
Students & Salary Earners Underserved
The scatter plot shows students and salary earners cluster in the top-right quadrant — high credit scores AND strong repayment rates. Yet these segments hold a small share of the portfolio, representing an untapped low-risk growth opportunity.
04
Ogun & Osun Show Regional Concentration Risk
The geospatial map reveals Ogun (₦1.82M NP) and Osun (₦1.79M NP) carry the highest non-performing balances relative to portfolio size. This may indicate weak local collection agents or borrower profile mismatches in those states.
05
Phone Purchase Loans Have Mixed Outcomes
Phone purchases appear in both the non-performing and written-off buckets. Unlike medical or school fee loans, phone assets depreciate rapidly and are hard to recover — making them a poor collateral basis for lending without IMEI registration.
06
Q1 2025 — Disbursements Outpaced Recovery
The monthly trend shows peak disbursements in Q1 2025 while the repayment rate line remained flat. New cohorts in this period are underperforming relative to earlier vintages, suggesting origination standards may have loosened to drive volume.

Strategic Recommendations

What Credmate Should Do

Based on the dashboard analysis, six evidence-based recommendations to address Credmate's structural credit quality vulnerabilities.

  • 🔒
    Restrict Self-Employed Loan Limits
    Cap loan amounts for self-employed applicants until income verification is strengthened. Introduce bank statement analysis or agency-verified cashflow checks before approving loans above ₦50,000 to this segment.
  • 📈
    Grow the Salary Earner Segment
    Salary earners and students show the best repayment behaviour in the scatter analysis. Credmate should develop employer-linked payroll deduction products for this segment, reducing collection friction and default risk simultaneously.
  • 📍
    Deploy Field Agents in Ogun & Osun
    The geographic concentration of non-performing loans in Ogun and Osun warrants dedicated recovery agents in these states. Consider partnering with local cooperative societies to improve borrower contact rates and reduce the NP balance by 30% within two quarters.
  • 📱
    Re-evaluate Phone Purchase Products
    Phone purchase loans appear in both NP and written-off buckets in the decomposition tree. Either require IMEI registration as collateral, shorten loan tenors for device purchases, or introduce device insurance as a mandatory loan condition.
  • 🏦
    Monitor Renmoney & Carbon Exposure
    The lender exposure chart shows Renmoney and Carbon carrying the largest share of the non-performing book. Renegotiate risk-sharing terms or rebalance disbursements toward better-performing lender partnerships like Branch and PalmCredit.
  • ⚡
    Implement Cohort-Level Performance Gates
    Q1 2025 saw peak disbursements but flat repayment rates. Credmate should implement monthly cohort monitoring: if a cohort's 30-day repayment rate falls below 60%, automatically pause new approvals until the vintage stabilises — protecting the book from further deterioration.
